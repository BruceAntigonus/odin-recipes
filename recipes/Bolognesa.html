Bolognese

Bolognese is a classic Italian meat sauce that originates from the city of Bologna. Itâ€™s traditionally made with ground beef or a mix of beef and pork

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef (or mix of beef and pork)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, finely chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes (or 2 tbsp tomato paste)
  • 1/2 cup red wine (optional)
  • 1/2 cup milk (optional)
  • 1 cup beef or chicken stock
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Fresh basil (optional, for garnish)
  • Parmesan cheese (optional, for serving)
  • Pasta (e.g., tagliatelle or spaghetti)
  1. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onions, carrots, and celery. Cook until the vegetables are soft, about 5-7 minutes.
  2. Add the garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the ground beef and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon as it cooks. Drain any excess fat if needed.
  4. Stir in the crushed tomatoes (or tomato paste), red wine (if using), and beef or chicken stock. Season with salt, pepper, and oregano.
  5. Lower the heat and simmer uncovered for at least 30 minutes, stirring occasionally. For richer flavor, simmer for 1-2 hours, adding more stock or water if needed.
  6. Stir in the milk at the end (if using) for extra creaminess. Adjust seasoning.
  7. Cook the pasta according to package instructions. Drain and toss with the Bolognese sauce.
  8. Serve with fresh basil and grated Parmesan cheese if desired.
