Brownie

A brownie is a classic American dessert that is rich, chocolatey, and dense. It's made from a mixture of melted chocolate or cocoa powder, butter, sugar, eggs, and flour, often with the addition of ingredients like nuts or chocolate chips.

  • 1/2 cup (115g) butter, melted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar (optional)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder (or 1 cup melted dark chocolate)
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder (optional, for a cakey texture)
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips (optional)
  • 1/2 cup nuts (optional, e.g., walnuts or pecans)
  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease and line an 8x8 inch (20x20 cm) baking pan with parchment paper.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine melted butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar (if using). Mix until smooth.
  3. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together flour, cocoa powder (or melted chocolate), baking powder (if using), and salt.
  5. Gradually fold the dry ingredients into the wet mixture until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.
  6. If desired, fold in chocolate chips and/or nuts for extra texture.
  7. Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan and spread evenly.
  8. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out with a few moist crumbs. For fudgier brownies, bake for less time.
  9. Let the brownies cool completely in the pan before cutting into squares and serving.
