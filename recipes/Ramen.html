Ramen

Ramen is a Japanese noodle soup dish that consists of Chinese-style wheat noodles served in a rich broth. The broth can vary—ranging from soy sauce-based (shoyu), miso-based, or pork-based (tonkotsu)—and is often flavored with ingredients like garlic, ginger, and soy sauce. Ramen is usually topped with slices of pork, soft-boiled eggs, nori (seaweed), and green onions.

  • 4 packs ramen noodles (wheat-based)
  • 4 cups chicken, pork, or vegetable broth
  • 2 tbsp miso paste
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, minced
  • 4 slices pork belly or chicken (optional)
  • 4 soft-boiled eggs
  • 4 sheets nori (seaweed)
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1/2 cup mushrooms (optional)
  • 1/4 cup bamboo shoots (optional)
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil (optional)
  • 1 tsp chili oil (optional)
  1. Cook the ramen noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a pot, heat the broth over medium heat. Add soy sauce, garlic, and ginger. Stir well and bring to a simmer.
  3. In a small bowl, dissolve the miso paste with a ladle of the hot broth, then stir the mixture back into the pot. Be sure the broth doesn't boil once the miso is added to avoid bitterness.
  4. Prepare the soft-boiled eggs by boiling them for 6 minutes. Remove and place in ice water, then peel.
  5. In a separate pan, cook the pork belly or chicken slices until browned and set aside.
  6. Divide the cooked ramen noodles into bowls. Pour the hot miso broth over the noodles.
  7. Top each bowl with slices of pork belly or chicken, soft-boiled egg halves, nori sheets, green onions, mushrooms, and bamboo shoots.
  8. Drizzle with sesame oil and chili oil (optional) for an extra layer of flavor.
  9. Serve hot and enjoy!
